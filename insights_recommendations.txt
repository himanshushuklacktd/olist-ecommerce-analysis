4 Key Insights

INSIGHT 1 — Revenue Growth
Brazil's e-commerce revenue grew from nearly zero in late 2016
to R$1.1M/month by late 2018 — a 15x growth in just 2 years,
indicating a rapidly maturing market.

INSIGHT 2 — Black Friday Effect
November 2017 recorded a 53% Month-on-Month revenue spike —
the highest single-month growth in the entire dataset —
directly driven by Black Friday sales activity.

INSIGHT 3 — Geographic Concentration
São Paulo state alone accounts for the majority of orders,
followed by Rio de Janeiro and Minas Gerais. The top 3 states
represent the Southeast region and drive ~60% of total volume.

INSIGHT 4 — Delivery Performance
8.06% of delivered orders arrived late, with an average
delivery time of 12.5 days. Late deliveries represent a
measurable customer experience risk at scale.


3 Business Recommendations

RECOMMENDATION 1 — Invest in Seasonal Campaigns
Black Friday drove a 53% revenue spike. Olist should plan
dedicated inventory, logistics, and marketing campaigns
for Q4 (Oct–Dec) to maximize this seasonal opportunity.

RECOMMENDATION 2 — Expand Logistics in Southeast
Since SP, RJ, and MG dominate order volume, Olist should
prioritize fulfillment center expansion and last-mile
delivery partnerships in the Southeast region to reduce
the 12.5-day average delivery time.

RECOMMENDATION 3 — Address Late Delivery Root Cause
8.06% late delivery rate should be investigated by seller
location and product category. Sellers outside Southeast
likely drive higher late rates — targeted SLA enforcement
can reduce churn and improve repeat purchase rates.
